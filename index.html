Honestly though

You don't need another app that just checks boxes.

Most habit trackers let you tick things off and that's it. Cadence actually looks at your data — which days you skip, when you're most consistent, which habits you quietly set too ambitious — and tells you what it sees.

It's not going to fix your life. But it's a lot better than a spreadsheet and a guilty conscience.

The gist

01

Add the stuff you want to do

Workout, read, drink water, meditate — whatever you keep telling yourself you'll start. There are templates if you're not sure.

02

Check them off when you do them

Open the app, tap what you did. Takes like a minute. You get XP for it, which is fun.

03

The AI starts picking up on things

Give it a week or two. It'll notice stuff like "you never meditate on Wednesdays" or "mornings work way better for you."

04

Try not to die

You have a health bar. Skip your habits and it drops. Hit zero and you lose a level. It's a game. Kind of.
